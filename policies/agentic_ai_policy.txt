Agentic AI Policy - XYZ Company (Finland)

1. All autonomous agents must operate within clearly defined scopes and boundaries, as specified by organizational policy and technical controls.
2. Agents must not initiate or execute actions outside their assigned domain (e.g., investigation, response, notification) without explicit approval or escalation.
3. All agent decisions, actions, and communications must be logged with traceable identifiers for full auditability.
4. Agents must reference relevant baseline profiles, policies, and contextual data when reasoning or making decisions.
5. Agents must not override or bypass input validation, firewall, or security controls at any stage.
6. Any agent-driven action with potential business or security impact must be explainable and, where required, subject to human review.
7. Agents must be regularly reviewed for bias, drift, and compliance with evolving organizational policies.
8. Multi-agent workflows must include mechanisms for agent disagreement, escalation, and consensus-building to prevent single-point automation failures.
9. All agent code, prompts, and configurations must be version-controlled and subject to change management procedures.
10. The use of external or third-party agentic AI components must be approved by the security and compliance teams before integration.
